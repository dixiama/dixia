Our project

Dixia is a school project aimed at improving educational inclusion by helping teachers identify and effectively manage learning difficulties such as dyslexia (which affects approximately 10-15% of the population), dyscalculia (present in around 5-7% of the population), and dysgraphia (which impacts 5-20% of students). The main idea is to raise awareness and simultaneously assist teachers by providing them with tools for their training as well as activities they can directly use with their students. This goal seeks to ensure that no student is left behind due to barriers that can be addressed in the classroom with the right tools.

Currently, 85% of teachers state that they are not sufficiently trained to tackle these areas in the classroom. Many times, they also do not have the necessary time to take training courses, or they tend to be paid, which limits their access to resources.

Our project focuses on providing free and accessible resources for all educators, with the goal of enhancing educational quality and promoting inclusion in the classrooms. We firmly believe that with the right tools, educators can make a significant impact on their students' lives, ensuring that every child has the opportunity to realize their full potential.

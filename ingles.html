LEARNING DIFFICULTIES
Specific Learning Difficulties are conditions that affect a person’s ability to acquire, process, or use information effectively. These difficulties may manifest in areas such as reading, writing, mathematics, attention, memory, organization, or problem-solving.
DIXIA
DYSLEXIA
Dyslexia is a learning disorder related to reading and writing, persistent and specific in nature, that occurs in children who do not present any physical, psychological, or socio-cultural disadvantages or unfavorable conditions, and whose origin appears to stem from a neurodevelopmental alteration.
DIXIA
DYSCALCULIA
Dyscalculia is a condition that makes it difficult to solve mathematical operations and tasks. Although less well-known than dyslexia, it is estimated to affect between 5% and 10% of people.
DIXIA
DYSGRAPHIA
It is a learning disorder that causes difficulties in the stroke, size, and shape of letters, making writing a demanding process if early intervention or appropriate tools are not provided.
